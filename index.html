Introduction

Hello! My name is Morgan Cselinacz and I am a graduate student at the University of Alberta, currently completing my Master of Arts in Digital Humanities. I have a Bachelor of Science in Psychology and a Certificate in Computer Game Development.

My current research focuses on how artificial intelligence is represented in media - specifically, video games - and how this affects general public perception. I also have an interest in game studies, narrative design, as well as user experience in games. I hope to work in the games industry once I finish my degree.

Welcome and please take a look at an overview of my experience, some projects I have worked on, as well as my CV.

Projects

Creator | Bleak Citizenship Society [working title]

A masquerade composed of bizarre people where the masks worn are the faces of other characters. The player must learn the ‘original’ identities of their fellow masquerade attenders through logic puzzles.

A current project I wish to develop into a full experience, Bleak Citizenship Society is meant to explore themes of identity, performativity, gender roles and fluidity, and how we deal with the fear of ourselves and of death.

Created for DH MLCS 499: Advanced Critical Game Design and Theory at the University of Alberta. Currently looking for collaborators.

Creator | In My Skin

You wake up in a room. There is an android there, watching you...

Designer and writer for the Twine experience In My Skin, which explores concepts such as artificial intelligence, humanity, time, identity, and self.

Created for DH 510: Digital Fictions at the University of Alberta.

Lead Writer | THEA.png

Voyeuristically explore someone else’s computer in a game that portrays the feelings of fear and control in an abusive relationship.

Lead writer for the puzzle video game THEA.png, which emulates the GUI of an old Windows XP computer and delves into the life of Thea, a woman in an abusive relationship. I wrote the story script and overlooked the writing of the game for consistency, aesthetic, tone, and impact. I also helped lead the team to meet deadlines and balance scope. Created in Unity.

Created for INTD 450: Computers and Games Capstone at the University of Alberta.

Designer | The Great Acorn Race

The animals of the forest have come together for the annual Acorn Race! Choose which animal you will be as you vie for fastest critter of the forest by building the best deck you can. Beat the other animals to the finish line and show the tortoise and the hare who the fastest animal really is!

Designer for The Great Acorn Race, a card-based resource building board game with a focus on balanced and strategic gameplay.

Created for INTD 350: Game Design Principles and Practice at the University of Alberta. Soon to be released to the public.

Writer, Producer | Mr. Catfish's Singles Retreat Event Extravaganza!!!

You - a robot named A13X - are invited to the exclusive romantic getaway run by Mr. Catfish. Here, will you find the meaning of true love, or will your dates be murdered before a connection can be made?

Writer and producer for the 2-D point-and-click adventure / dating simulator / murder mystery game Mr. Catfish's Singles Retreat Event Extravaganza!!!. I wrote the script, branching dialogue paths, different endings, and led the project for scope, deadlines, consistency, and quality. Created in RPG Maker MV.

Created for CMPUT 250: Computers and Games at the University of Alberta. Nominated for Excellence in Writing and Story, Audio Achievement, Best Gameplay Design, Game of the Year, and won Excellence in Art and Design at the Computer Game Development Certificate's 2019 Awards Ceremony.
