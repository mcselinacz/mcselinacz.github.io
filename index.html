About Me

Hello! My name is Morgan Cselinacz and I am a graduate student at the University of Alberta, currently completing my Master of Arts in Digital Humanities. I have a Bachelor of Science in Psychology and a Certificate in Computer Game Development.

My current research focuses on how artificial intelligence is represented in media - specifically, video games - and how this affects general public perception. I also have an interest in game studies, narrative design, as well as user experience in games. I hope to work in the games industry once I finish my degree.

    Video Games, Electronic Literature, and Emotional Content: What does it do? Does it do anything? Let’s find out!!

    Video games and electronic literature are able to simplify, exaggerate, and explore issues that happen in real life. Abuse, racism, sexism, coming out to family, and anxieties related to death, the future, and artificial intelligence (AI) are just a few of the topics that are covered by these interactive mediums. I will particularly look at how AI is viewed in electronic literature and video games, and how these representations play into fears and anxieties surrounding this topic.
